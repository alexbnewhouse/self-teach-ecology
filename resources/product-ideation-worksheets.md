# Product Ideation Worksheets

Supporting resource for [Module 13: Personal Rewilding Product](../curriculum/13-rewilding-product-development.md).

Use these structured worksheets to develop and compare product concepts. Fill them out by hand or in a markdown copy—the structure matters more than the format.

---

## Worksheet 1: Concept Definition Card

Complete one card per product concept (minimum three in Phase 2).

```
CONCEPT NAME: _______________________________________________

ONE-SENTENCE PITCH:
"A [product type] that helps [user] to [action] by [mechanism]."

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

TARGET USER (primary):
- Who are they?
- What do they already do related to ecology/gardening?
- What's their comfort level with technology?
- How much time do they have?
- How much space do they have?

TARGET USER (secondary):
- Who else benefits from this product?
- How do their needs differ from the primary user?

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

THE PROBLEM:
- What specific frustration or gap does this address?
- What do people currently do instead? (Existing alternatives)
- Why are existing alternatives insufficient?

THE ECOLOGICAL INSIGHT:
- What ecological knowledge makes this product better than
  what a non-ecologist would build?
- Which curriculum modules directly inform this?
  □ Module 1 (Foundations)    □ Module 5 (Quantitative)
  □ Module 2 (Conservation)  □ Module 6 (Genetics)
  □ Module 3 (Rewilding)     □ Module 7 (Climate)
  □ Module 4 (Landscape)     □ Module 8 (Policy)
  □ Restoration Track        □ Computational Track
  □ Systems Track            □ Other: ___________

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

CORE FEATURES (MVP — maximum 3):
1. ___________________________________________________
2. ___________________________________________________
3. ___________________________________________________

FEATURES FOR LATER (post-MVP):
- ___________________________________________________
- ___________________________________________________
- ___________________________________________________

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

SCALE ADAPTATION:
How does this work at each scale?

Containers/balcony:  ________________________________________
Small yard:          ________________________________________
Suburban lot:        ________________________________________
Large property:      ________________________________________
Rural/acreage:       ________________________________________

───────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

DATA REQUIREMENTS:
- What data does the product need to function?
- What data does the user provide?
- What data does the product generate?
- What external APIs or databases are needed?

REVENUE MODEL (initial guess):
□ Free + premium tier    □ One-time purchase
□ Subscription           □ Affiliate/referral
□ Hardware + software    □ Marketplace
□ Freemium + data        □ Other: ___________

BIGGEST RISK:
What's the single most likely reason this product fails?
___________________________________________________________

UNFAIR ADVANTAGE:
What do YOU specifically bring that makes this harder for
others to replicate?
___________________________________________________________
```

## Worksheet 6: Revenue Sanity Check

Quick math to test whether your revenue model can work.

```
CONCEPT: _______________________________________________
REVENUE MODEL: _________________________________________

COST SIDE:
  Hosting / infrastructure:     $______ /month
  Data source costs:            $______ /month
  Your time (opportunity cost): $______ /month
  Other costs:                  $______ /month
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Total monthly cost:           $______ /month

REVENUE SIDE:
  Price per unit/subscription:  $______
  Realistic users (year 1):     ______
  Conversion rate (if freemium): ______%
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  Monthly revenue at year 1:    $______ /month

  Break-even users needed:      ______
  Is that realistic? □ Yes □ Stretch □ No

PASSIVE INCOME TEST:
  Hours/week to maintain after launch: ______
  Revenue per hour of maintenance:     $______
  Is this actually passive? □ Yes □ Mostly □ No

MISSION TEST:
  Could you give this away free and still feel good? □ Yes □ No
  Does charging money make the product BETTER?       □ Yes □ No
  (e.g., funds better data, sustains development)
```

## How to Use These Worksheets

1. **Phase 2, Week 5**: Fill out Worksheet 1 for three concepts
2. **Phase 2, Week 6**: Complete Worksheets 2 and 3 for your top two concepts
3. **Phase 2, Week 7**: Do Worksheets 4, 5, and 6 for your selected concept
4. **Phase 2, Week 8**: Complete Worksheet 7 before moving to Phase 3
5. **Revisit**: Update worksheets as your understanding deepens through prototyping
