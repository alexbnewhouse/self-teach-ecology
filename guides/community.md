## üë• Finding Study Partners

### Strategies to Find Accountability Partners

1. **Post in r/ecology or r/conservation:**
   - "Seeking study partner for self-taught conservation ecology"
   - Specify timezone, weekly time commitment, communication preferences

2. **Form a Virtual Study Group:**
   - Weekly video calls to discuss readings
   - Shared document for notes and questions
   - Peer review each other's projects
   - Tools: Zoom, Discord, Google Meet

3. **Study Together Platforms:**
   - **Focusmate:** [https://www.focusmate.com/](https://www.focusmate.com/) - Virtual coworking
   - **Study Stream:** Virtual study rooms
   - **Discord Study Servers:** Many have video study rooms

4. **Local Environmental Groups:**
   - Check Meetup.com for ecology/conservation groups
   - Connect with local university ecology departments
   - Volunteer with conservation organizations

### Sample Study Group Structure

**Weekly Meeting Agenda (1.5 hours):**
1. Check-in & progress updates (15 min)
2. Discuss assigned readings (30 min)
3. Work through exercise or problem together (30 min)
4. Set goals for next week (15 min)

**Between Meetings:**
- Shared reading schedule
- Post questions to group chat
- Share interesting articles or findings

- Come prepared with specific questions
- Respect their time
- Follow their advice and report back
- Express gratitude

## üí° Tips for Engaging with Communities

1. **Give Before You Take:**
   - Share interesting papers or news
   - Help others when you can
   - Contribute to discussions

2. **Be Specific in Questions:**
   - "How do I design this specific sampling scheme?" rather than "How do I do ecology?"
   - Show what you've already tried

3. **Follow Community Norms:**
   - Read community guidelines
   - Search before posting duplicate questions
   - Be respectful and professional

4. **Build Relationships:**
   - Engage with same people over time
   - Remember details about their work
   - Celebrate others' successes

5. **Contribute Your Unique Perspective:**
   - Your political science background is valuable
   - Your data science skills are needed
   - Your questions help others learn
