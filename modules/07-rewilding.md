# Module 7: Rewilding Theory & Practice

**Duration:** 8 weeks  
**Effort:** 15-20 hours/week

## üìã Overview

Rewilding represents a paradigm shift in conservation, emphasizing restoration of natural processes and ecological complexity rather than maintaining static ecosystems. This module explores the theory, practice, and controversies surrounding rewilding as a conservation strategy.

## üéØ Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

1. Define and distinguish different approaches to rewilding
2. Understand the role of trophic interactions in ecosystem function
3. Evaluate evidence for trophic cascades and ecosystem effects of rewilding
4. Assess feasibility and challenges of rewilding projects
5. Understand the social, political, and ethical dimensions of rewilding
6. Design a rewilding strategy for a specific context

## üíª Exercises & Activities

### Week 1-2: Understanding Rewilding

**Exercise 7.1: Defining Rewilding**
- Compare and contrast definitions of rewilding from different sources
- Map the spectrum from passive rewilding to Pleistocene rewilding
- Analyze how rewilding differs from traditional restoration
- **Deliverable:** Conceptual framework document with comparative table

**Exercise 7.2: Historical Baselines**
- For a chosen region, research historical fauna and flora (pre-human or early Holocene)
- Identify species extirpated and reasons for loss
- Assess which species could potentially be restored
- Discuss appropriate temporal baselines for rewilding
- **Deliverable:** Historical ecology report with timeline

### Week 3-4: Trophic Rewilding & Cascades

**Exercise 7.3: Trophic Cascade Analysis**
- Review evidence for a well-documented trophic cascade (e.g., wolves in Yellowstone, sea otters in kelp forests)
- Critically evaluate the strength of evidence
- Map the cascade through trophic levels
- Discuss alternative explanations and confounding factors
- **Deliverable:** Critical analysis with food web diagram

**Exercise 7.4: Predator Reintroduction Assessment**
- Select a region and a candidate apex predator for reintroduction
- Assess ecological conditions: prey availability, habitat quality, connectivity
- Predict ecosystem effects based on trophic cascade theory
- Identify potential conflicts and challenges
- **Deliverable:** Feasibility assessment report

### Week 5-6: Rewilding in Practice

**Exercise 7.5: Case Study Deep Dive**
Choose one major rewilding project and analyze in depth:
- **Options:** Yellowstone to Yukon (Y2Y), Oostvaardersplassen (Netherlands), Knepp Estate (UK), Iber√° Wetlands (Argentina), Pleistocene Park (Russia)
- Describe goals, methods, timeline, and outcomes
- Evaluate success using ecological and social metrics
- Identify lessons learned
- **Deliverable:** Comprehensive case study report (2500 words)

**Exercise 7.6: Passive vs. Active Rewilding**
- Compare a passive rewilding site with an active rewilding site
- Document vegetation succession and fauna colonization
- Analyze costs, benefits, and outcomes of each approach
- Discuss when each approach is most appropriate
- **Deliverable:** Comparative analysis with cost-benefit assessment

### Week 7-8: Social Dimensions & Planning

**Exercise 7.7: Stakeholder Analysis**
- For a hypothetical or real rewilding proposal, identify all stakeholders
- Analyze interests, concerns, and power dynamics
- Develop stakeholder engagement strategy
- Address potential conflicts (human-wildlife conflict, land use, livelihoods)
- **Deliverable:** Stakeholder analysis matrix with engagement plan

**Exercise 7.8: Rewilding Ethics**
- Explore ethical questions in rewilding:
  - Should we prioritize native species or ecological function?
  - Is de-extinction justified for rewilding?
  - Who decides what "wild" means?
  - How do we balance human and wildlife needs?
- Develop your own ethical framework for rewilding decisions
- **Deliverable:** Ethics position paper (1500 words)

## üåç Capstone Project

**Project 7: Rewilding Strategy Design**

Design a comprehensive rewilding strategy for a real landscape:

1. **Site Selection & Baseline Assessment**
   - Choose a site (abandoned farmland, degraded ecosystem, etc.)
   - Document current ecological conditions
   - Research historical ecology

2. **Goals & Vision**
   - Define rewilding objectives (species, processes, ecosystem services)
   - Identify target species for reintroduction or recovery
   - Set temporal and spatial scales

3. **Implementation Plan**
   - Phased approach (passive regeneration, species reintroductions, habitat modification)
   - Timeline with milestones
   - Resource requirements (land, funding, personnel)

4. **Monitoring & Adaptive Management**
   - Indicators of success (ecological, social, economic)
   - Monitoring protocols
   - Adaptive management framework

5. **Social Integration**
   - Stakeholder engagement strategy
   - Economic opportunities (ecotourism, payments for ecosystem services)
   - Education and outreach

6. **Risk Assessment & Mitigation**
   - Ecological risks (invasive species, disease)
   - Social risks (human-wildlife conflict, land use disputes)
   - Mitigation strategies

**Deliverable:** Complete rewilding strategy (5000+ words) with maps, timelines, and budget
