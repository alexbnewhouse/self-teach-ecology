# Module 8: Conservation Policy & Social Science

**Duration:** 6 weeks  
**Effort:** 15-20 hours/week

## üìã Overview

Conservation is as much a social and political challenge as it is a biological one. This module bridges natural and social sciences, exploring how policy, governance, economics, and human behavior shape conservation outcomes. For students with political science backgrounds, this module connects your existing expertise to conservation contexts.

## üéØ Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you should be able to:

1. Understand major environmental policy frameworks and instruments
2. Analyze institutional arrangements for conservation governance
3. Apply concepts from environmental economics to conservation
4. Evaluate community-based conservation approaches
5. Understand human dimensions of conservation (attitudes, behavior, conflict)
6. Assess the role of power, politics, and justice in conservation
7. Design policy interventions for conservation challenges

## üíª Exercises & Activities

### Week 1-2: Policy Frameworks & Institutions

**Exercise 8.1: Policy Instrument Analysis**
- Compare policy instruments: regulation, economic incentives, voluntary agreements
- For a conservation problem, design 3 policy approaches using different instruments
- Analyze effectiveness, efficiency, equity, and political feasibility of each
- **Deliverable:** Policy instrument comparison matrix with recommendations

**Exercise 8.2: International Conservation Agreements**
- Review major agreements: CBD, CITES, Ramsar, World Heritage, Paris Agreement
- For one agreement, analyze: goals, mechanisms, compliance, effectiveness
- Identify gaps and propose reforms
- **Deliverable:** Critical analysis of international conservation agreement

**Exercise 8.3: Common-Pool Resource Management**
- Apply Ostrom's design principles to a conservation commons
- Analyze a case of successful or failed commons management
- Identify institutional conditions for sustainable governance
- **Deliverable:** Commons governance case study analysis

### Week 3-4: Economics & Incentives

**Exercise 8.4: Ecosystem Service Valuation**
- Choose an ecosystem and identify its services
- Apply valuation methods: market pricing, replacement cost, contingent valuation
- Discuss strengths and limitations of economic valuation for conservation
- Critique the "ecosystem services" framing
- **Deliverable:** Valuation report with critical reflection

**Exercise 8.5: Payment for Ecosystem Services (PES) Design**
- Design a PES scheme for a specific context (e.g., forest conservation, watershed protection)
- Define: who pays, who receives, for what, how much, monitoring
- Address additionality, leakage, and permanence
- Consider social equity implications
- **Deliverable:** PES program design document

**Exercise 8.6: Cost-Benefit Analysis**
- Conduct cost-benefit analysis of a conservation intervention
- Include ecological, economic, and social costs and benefits
- Discuss discount rates and intergenerational equity
- Address non-monetary values
- **Deliverable:** Cost-benefit analysis with ethical discussion

### Week 5-6: Human Dimensions & Justice

**Exercise 8.7: Community-Based Conservation Evaluation**
- Review a community-based conservation project (CBC or ICDPs)
- Evaluate using multiple criteria: conservation outcomes, livelihood impacts, empowerment, equity
- Analyze power dynamics and decision-making processes
- Identify success factors and challenges
- **Deliverable:** CBC project evaluation report

**Exercise 8.8: Human-Wildlife Conflict Analysis**
- Choose a conflict context (e.g., carnivore attacks on livestock, crop raiding)
- Map stakeholders, interests, and power relations
- Analyze root causes (ecological, economic, social, cultural)
- Design multi-faceted intervention strategy
- **Deliverable:** Conflict analysis and intervention design

**Exercise 8.9: Conservation Justice Audit**
- Select a conservation area or project
- Analyze justice dimensions: distributional (costs/benefits), procedural (participation), recognition (rights, knowledge systems)
- Identify injustices and marginalized groups
- Propose reforms to improve justice outcomes
- **Deliverable:** Conservation justice assessment

## üèõÔ∏è Capstone Project

**Project 8: Policy Brief & Advocacy Strategy**

Develop a comprehensive policy brief and advocacy strategy for a conservation issue:

1. **Problem Definition**
   - Describe conservation problem with data and evidence
   - Explain policy context and current approaches
   - Identify policy gaps or failures

2. **Policy Analysis**
   - Review existing policies and their effectiveness
   - Analyze institutional landscape and governance arrangements
   - Identify key actors and power dynamics

3. **Policy Recommendations**
   - Propose specific policy interventions (2-3 options)
   - Justify recommendations with evidence
   - Address implementation feasibility
   - Consider equity and justice implications

4. **Advocacy Strategy**
   - Identify target audiences (policymakers, public, media)
   - Develop key messages and framing
   - Plan advocacy tactics (lobbying, campaigns, coalition-building)
   - Anticipate opposition and counter-arguments

5. **Monitoring & Evaluation**
   - Propose indicators for policy success
   - Design evaluation framework

**Deliverable:** 
- Policy brief (2000 words, accessible to policymakers)
- Advocacy strategy document (2000 words)
- Optional: Create infographic or slide deck for advocacy
